# Hello again

Hello again to a future me, and to an unknown you.
I recently logged into my Amazon Web Services account and exported the databases of a number of old blogs.

There were a number of sites hosted on node for myself, friends, (my mother).
On many of the blogs, I wrote the first entry; excited for the person that I provisioned the platform for ("your **admin** here, get ready for a great blog").
Nostalgia filled, this is me doing the same, knowing I'll one day look back at this post and feel _something_.[^gyri]

Let's find out what that feeling is unknown reader (future Dylan you likely already know?), but I'll state my intention.
I just want to record things I find interesting, and put them out into the universe.
If the universe doesn't find my writings interesting, then I'll hopefully adapt and find more interesting things, or resign to being boring.
But I'm into [exploring][find me climbing, running, diving, flying], [data visualization][marimo, observable js, webgl, historical viz like du bois], [math][computation, diiferential equations, optimization], [science][space! did my PhD material science adjacent], [tech][compilers, functional programming, nix, ML, and passe but agents _are_ kinda cool], and might dabble in short stories; we'll see where things go.

A note on the site: this is a `.dm` domain which is the [country TLD](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/.dm) of Dominica- the island I grew up on and affectionately call home.
I only realized last year, my initials, <u>D</u>ylan <u>M</u>adisetti are also appropriate here.
Unfortunately, `.dm` is not as popular as Anguilla's `.ai` at 10% of their [GDP](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/.ai)[^slidein], but happy to give our [nic.dm](https://nic.dm) some business.


I knew it was time again to start webloggin' and here we are.
Some other blogs I'm going to look to for inspiration:
 - [rreusser](https://rreusser.github.io/)
 - coworkers [Micheal](https://mchav.github.io/) (mchav) and [trevor](https://trevorma.nz/about) (he doesn't capitalize the t)

There are others, but I feel like these are relatively hidden gems.
